# v11 Figure, Table and Box Plan

## Figure 1. 领域评价路线图

Purpose: show why the Review evaluates evidence, design principles and developability requirements rather than listing methods.

Panels:
- A. Candidate generation: sequence-conditioned, structure-conditioned, full-atom and cross-chirality generation.
- B. Evidence grading: affinity, experimental structure, cell function, animal or PK/PD, CMC.
- C. Reusable design principles: epitope definition, split design, negative design, conformational pre-organization, presentation mode, experimental testing flow.
- D. Preclinical-development milestones: standard data, developability panel, HLA/antigen safety, open code and weights.

## Figure 2. AI 多肽设计任务地图

Purpose: separate linear peptide, constrained peptide, D-peptide, macrocycle and pMHC/TCR-like recognition tasks.

Panels:
- A. Inputs: sequence, target structure, epitope, macrocyclization constraint, pMHC complex.
- B. Outputs: peptide sequence, macrocycle conformation, D-peptide, recognition module, cellular component.
- C. Failure modes: flexibility, degradation, chirality, permeability, off-target antigen recognition.
- D. Required validation: affinity, structure, cell assay, animal or PK/PD, CMC.

## Figure 3. Evidence ladder from computation to drug candidate

Purpose: prevent evidence-level conflation.

Panels:
- A. Computational generation.
- B. In vitro binding.
- C. Experimental structure.
- D. Cell function.
- E. Animal or PK/PD evidence.
- F. Developability and CMC.

Use manuscript Table 2 to compare conditional generation, structure-sequence coupling, full-atom representation, cross-chirality design, negative design and reproducibility.
