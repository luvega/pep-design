- `raw/fulltext_md/2021-rational-design-of-potent-peptide-inhibitors-of-the-pd-1-pd-l1-interacti-1ecaf700.md:59` ABSTRACT: Peptides have potential to be developed into immune checkpoint inhibitors, but the target interfaces are difficult to inhibit. Here, we explored an approach to mimic the binding surface of PD-1 to design inhibitors. Mimicking native PD-1 resulted in a mimetic with no activity. However, mimicking an affinity-optimized PD-1 resulted in the peptide mimetic MOPD-1 that displayed nanomolar affinity to PD-L1 and could inhibit PD1:PD-L1 interactions in both protein- and cell-based assays.
