## Evidence Note
Peptides are fundamental regulators of metabolism, with several already developed as drugs, including glucagon-like peptide-1-based peptide therapeutics for diabetes and obesity. Despite their established importance, our understanding of their biosynthesis, modifications, receptor interactions, and signaling pathways remains incomplete. Advances in peptidomics and proteomics, particularly mass spectrometry, have facilitated peptide discovery and characterization, revealing novel roles for known peptides and uncovering previously unrecognized post-translational modifications. With the increasing prevalence of metabolic diseases driven by obesity, understanding the regulatory functions of peptide hormones has significant therapeutic potential.
